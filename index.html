Aadi Shah

I’m a mechanical engineering student at the University of Southern California with a minor in business finance. I like building hardware systems that have to work outside of clean, controlled environments.

I’m originally from Singapore, and growing up there shaped many of the problems I’m interested in—dense cities, heavy infrastructure demands, and systems that need to function reliably despite environmental and operational constraints.

What I Work On

Most of my work revolves around hands-on mechanical design and hardware system integration. I’m drawn to projects where hardware decisions directly impact performance, reliability, and user behavior.

  • Mechanical design and CAD (SolidWorks, Siemens NX)
  • Hardware-focused system integration
  • Rapid prototyping and 3D printing
  • Designing for deployment, maintenance, and failure modes

What I’m Currently Building

I’m currently working on a hardware startup project focused on 3D-printed, weather-capable drones for traffic monitoring in India. The goal is to build platforms that can operate reliably during monsoon seasons, where rain, wind, humidity, and poor visibility cause most off-the-shelf systems to fail.

Alongside this, I stay involved in aircraft, vehicle, and propulsion-related projects to continue building system-level intuition—from conceptual aircraft design to drivetrain and autonomous vehicle work.

Outside of Work

Outside of engineering, I spend a lot of time around vehicles and outdoors. I’m a big car and aviation guy, enjoy road biking and the gym, and like getting outside to hike when I can. I also love cooking in my free time.

  • Road biking and training at the gym
  • Cars, planes, and Formula 1
  • Hiking and being outdoors
  • Cooking and experimenting with food
