Aadi Shah

I’m a mechanical engineering student at the University of Southern California with a minor in business finance. I’m interested in building real hardware systems that have to work outside of controlled environments and under real operational constraints.

I’m originally from Singapore, and growing up there has shaped many of the problems I’m interested in—dense cities, high infrastructure demands, and systems that need to function reliably despite environmental and logistical challenges.

What I Work On

Most of my work revolves around hands-on mechanical design and hardware system integration. I enjoy projects where hardware decisions meaningfully affect performance, reliability, and user behavior.

  • Mechanical design and CAD (SolidWorks, Siemens NX)
  • Hardware-focused system integration
  • Rapid prototyping and 3D printing
  • Designing for deployment, maintenance, and failure modes

What I’m Currently Building

I’m currently working on a hardware startup project focused on 3D-printed, weather-capable drones for traffic monitoring in India. The goal is to build platforms that can operate reliably during monsoon seasons, where rain, wind, humidity, and poor visibility cause most off-the-shelf systems to fail.

Alongside this, I stay involved in aircraft, vehicle, and propulsion-related projects to continue building system-level intuition—from conceptual aircraft design to drivetrain and autonomous vehicle work.

Outside of Work

Outside of engineering, I spend a lot of time around vehicles and outdoors. I’m a big car and aviation enthusiast, and I enjoy understanding how complex mechanical systems behave under real operating conditions.

  • Road biking and staying active at the gym
  • Cars, planes, and Formula 1
  • Hiking and spending time outdoors
  • Cooking and experimenting with food in my free time
