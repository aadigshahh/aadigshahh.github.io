Autonomous Vehicle Design Project (King of the Hill)

Design Lead · March 2024 – May 2024

This project involved the design and construction of a small autonomous vehicle built to compete in a torque- and speed-driven hill-climbing challenge. The vehicle was required to repeatedly climb a steep incline under dynamic load conditions while maintaining mechanical reliability.

My focus was on mechanical system design and integration rather than advanced autonomy. The project emphasized real-world constraints such as drivetrain alignment, structural stiffness, wiring robustness, and repeatable performance under repeated testing.

Key Contributions

  • Designed and reinforced a wooden chassis to reduce flex under high torque loads
  • Optimized placement of motors, battery, Arduino, and sensors for stability and accessibility
  • Improved drivetrain alignment to reduce mechanical losses and inconsistent behavior
  • Debugged performance issues caused by wiring movement, voltage drop, and mounting flexibility
  • Tuned Arduino control logic once mechanical reliability was achieved

Design Focus

  • Mechanical robustness under dynamic loading
  • Drivetrain consistency and alignment
  • System-level integration of mechanical and electrical components
  • Iterative debugging through physical testing
