Autonomous Vehicle Design Project

“King of the Hill” · Spring 2024

This project involved designing and building a small autonomous vehicle for a hill-climbing challenge where performance depended on torque, traction, and mechanical reliability rather than advanced autonomy. The vehicle was tested repeatedly under dynamic loading and competitive conditions.

My focus was primarily on the mechanical system design and integration—making sure the chassis, drivetrain, and component placement could survive repeated runs and perform consistently.

Design Focus

  • Chassis stiffness and structural reinforcement under torque load
  • Drivetrain alignment to reduce mechanical losses
  • Component placement for stability, accessibility, and wiring robustness
  • Iterative debugging through physical testing
Full vehicle CAD assembly used to evaluate packaging, drivetrain layout, and subsystem integration.
