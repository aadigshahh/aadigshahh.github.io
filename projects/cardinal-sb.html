Cardinal SB – Aircraft Design

AME 261 · Conceptual Aircraft Design · Spring 2025

Cardinal SB was a team-based conceptual aircraft design project focused on a long-range, large-cabin business jet capable of operating from shorter runways while maintaining high-subsonic cruise performance. The project emphasized realistic constraints, trade studies, and system-level decision making rather than purely theoretical analysis.

I was involved throughout the design process, contributing to configuration decisions, performance analysis, propulsion trade studies, and technical documentation as part of a multi-member design team.

Technical Focus Areas

  • Aircraft configuration and layout trade studies
  • Wing and tail sizing using stability and performance constraints
  • Propulsion selection and thrust-to-weight considerations
  • Range, payload, and takeoff performance analysis
  • Integration of cabin, structural, and systems requirements
