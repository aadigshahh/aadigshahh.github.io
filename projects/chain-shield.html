Chain Shield Cover

USC Racing (SAE) · Mechanical Engineering Exposure

As part of the USC Racing team, I participated in early design discussions and integration reviews for a drivetrain chain shield. The goal was to protect rotating components while maintaining proper chain clearance and ease of assembly.

My involvement focused on understanding real-world constraints—packaging, alignment, manufacturability, and team integration—rather than owning the final part design.

USC Racing vehicle context showing drivetrain packaging and integration constraints.

Key Considerations

  • Chain clearance under dynamic motion
  • Mounting alignment and tolerance stack-up
  • Ease of assembly and serviceability
  • Safety and competition compliance
